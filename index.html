FPV Drone - 3D Modeling & Frame Architecture

Designed and modeled a fully custom 5 inch FPV drone frame in Autodesk Fusion using a parametric workflow to enable rapid iteration and geometry refinement. Modeled base plates, motor mounts, vibration resistance mounts with manufacturing constraints. Optimized tolerances for 3D printing, material shrinkage to ensure repeatable assembly.

Redesigned frame from acrylic to carbon fiber reinforced nylon (PA6-CF) to improve stiffness and impact resistance. Performed finite element analysis (FEA) and vibration tests to reduce arm flex and flight instability. Adjusted base plate thickness and motor arm thickness to 0.5 in, infill density to cut down on print time and balance strength and thickness.

Lunar Bucket Excavation System

Designed and modeled a bucket excavation system in Autodesk Fusion for NASA's Lunabotics competition, developing a lightweight regolith (space dirt) collection system for load distribution and material transfer. Engineered a linear actuator tilting system that controls the discharge of regolith with precise positioning and structural stability.

Performed material selection based on tensile strength, coefficient of friction and FEA test to simulate the lunar excavation loads. Refined geometry to reduce mass while maintaining structural integrity, imrpoving stiffness to weight ratio and increase scoring potential.

Underwater ROV -- Mechanical Design & Controls

Designed and fabricated a mechanical claw assembly in Autodesk Fusion designing a gear reduction system to increase output torque and gripping force for underwater object retrieval. Prototyped and tested 3D printed components and achieving a 10% reduction in motor current draw while increasing grip strength.

Developed vectored thrust control logic (VEXcode) to map inputs into multi thruster force vectors, allowing six degree of freedom maneuvering and smooth stabilization. Optimized thrust distribution and mechanical alignment to improve underwater handling and responsiveness.
