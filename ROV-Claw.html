ROV Claw & Motor Design

Designed and implemented a torque amplified underwater robotic claw for object retrieval, using gearing, and prototyping. Also designed vectored control for precise movement underwater.

Claw Mechanism Design

The underwater claw was designed in Autodesk Fusion to enable reliable object retrieval and manipulation during competition. The gripper can grasp objects up to 10 cm in diameter and uses a 3:1 gear reduction to increase torque while reducing motor strain. The claw rotates as well which needed a 52:1 reduction providing enough torque. Prototyping utilized 3D-printed PLA components allowed multiple design iterations to allow more flexible gear alignment, shaft choice, and claw geometry. Also since I was CEO I was able to choose bilge pump motors which were cheap and removed the need for servos while still achieving reliable actuation without the need for waterproofing.

Control Logic

Vectored thrust control algorithms were implemented using VEXcode to translate joystick inputs into coordinated thrust outputs across multiple motors. This enabled smooth six degree of freedom (6-DOF) maneuvering and precise vehicle positioning during underwater tasks.

System Integration & impact

The final claw system successfully integrated mechanical transmission and ROV navigation into a cohesive system. The combination of torque amplified gripping and vectored thrust as well as leadership advising allowed my team to have a precise robot to complete tasks underwater and compete at the highest level for this competition (MATE ROV World Championship).

Key Engineering outcomes included:

  • Torque amplification through gear reduction
  • Reduced motor power consumption during gripping
  • Improved underwater manipulation precision
  • Reliable multi axis vehicle control for object retrieval tasks
