Lunar Bucket Excavation System

Designed a lightweight regolith excavation and discharge mechanism for NASA's Lunabotics competition, optimizing load transfer, mass efficiency and controlled material release.

Excavation System Design & Modeling

Developed a bucket excavation system in Autodesk Fusion, modeling mounting interfaces, screw holes and regolith contact geometry. The design was optimized to maximize material collection while maintaining a low coefficient of friction and light overall mass.

Many design iterations where presented in the group from how to store and release the regolith but a bucket and dumping mechanism was chose because of the reliability and industry standard in these types of machines. Load cases were estimated based on simulated regolith resistance forces, and mounting brackets reinforced to withstand peak digging forces without excessive deflection. Peak stress was reduced by 22% from original designs while decreasing system mass by 9%.

Structural Analysis & Optimization

  • Load Transfer efficiency.
  • Pivot stress reduction.
  • Friction minimized at joints.
  • Mass reduction without structural compromise.

Linear Actuation & Discharge Mechanism

Engineered a linear actuator driven system to control bucket discharge angle and ensure reliable regolith release into dump zone. Two actuators considered but lack of electronics only allowed one allocated to dumping system. This made managing the range and calibration more important as to not overstress the actuator.

Testing, Validation & impact

prototype components were fabricated and tested to evaluate joint rigidity and Actuation reliability. FEA was especially useful in helping my team determining which material was most useful in weight, coefficient of friction and tensile strength.
